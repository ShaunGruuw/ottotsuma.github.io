Rouge Overview

Rouges are a class that allow the user to absorb the spirit of their kills into themselves and use this to improve. This is often why Rouges make good hunters, scouts, assassins, investigators, gardeners, and so much more. They use their animal (or what ever else the absorbed) adaptations to their advantage.

Poison

Affects like poison, fire, fear can be resisted or your target might even have immunity to such an affect. This immunity refers to normal type poisons, rouges and other classes that specialises in such affects will always have skills/abilities or otherways to circumnavigate such resistances.

Race

Race does not have a large affect towards your class, all races can pick the rouge class. A few notible racial bonuses related to rouges are:

  • Drell - bonuses to camouflage
  • Dark eleves - bonuses to camouflage in the dark
  • Krogan - Unable to sneak
  • Argonian - bonuses to poisons, resistance to poison

Levels

  • Level 0 - 50

    0 - Starting skills unlocked, 10 - Gain spirit eating ability, 30 - basic skills unlocked(cap raised from 0 to 3), 50 - Advance spirit (Plus increase chance in spirit eating success).

  • Level 51 - 100

    70 - Unlock Spirit Projection (different from summoned creatures), 80 - intermediate skills unlocked (cap raised from 3 to 6), 100 - Class advancement

  • Class advancement

    As with other classes your advance class choices will depend on your starting class (Rouge), Spirit absorbed (At time of advance). If you pick a unique/quest class you cannot also pick an advanced class. (eg. You cannot be Saint and Vishkanya at the same time.)

Basic Rouge Skills

  • Stealth, Every point increases the duration by 10 seconds. However it also increases the cool down.
  • Dual Wield, Each point reduces the dual wielding penalty, and eventually it a buff.
  • Preparations, Can set up basic traps
  • Shadowstep, Move though the shadows instantly
  • Stealth Attack, An attack launched from stealth for bonus damage
  • Venom, adds poison to physical attacks
  • "Creature"-Venom, Ignores a % of poison resistance
  • Backslash, jump over a target and stab them in the back
  • Throwing Knife, throws a knife
  • Cloak and Dagger, short teleport without breaking sneak
  • Gag Order, silence target while active (short debuff on target per point)
  • Rupture Tendons, cut the targets tendons reducing their move speed
  • Spinning Edge, an attack
  • Gust Slash, an attack
  • Aeolian Edge, an attack, bonus from rear
  • Shadow Fang, an attack
  • Fleet of Foot, increase move speed
  • All fours, reduce damage from falling

Spirits

Spirits are not ghosts, they are not even powerful by themselves. Spirits give access to skills/powers/abilities & spells, this is why rouges choose to let spirits in.

A spirit can be obtained by eating the heart of the prey. While not always successful, it can grant the rouge access to that prey`s abilities. Eg. An eagle`s spirit can grant access to the "eagle eyes" skill and other stat boosts. Below are some of a few spirit examples:

Roles

Rouges often make up the physical damage roles in a party, they usually act as dps both ranged and melee. Rouges often get support skills for an adventuring party and are often seen in this role as well. Below are a few build examples:

  • Range dps build, Spirit: Grat (seed spitter), With a crossbow/bow any ranged weapons, the Grat bonuses help towards ranged accuracy and speed. It`s special skill to spit seeds from your mouth also count as a physical ranged attack. While not a commonly picked spirit, the advantage is that you always have a ranged weapon even while unarmed, So those ranged bonuses don`t go to waste.
  • Tank, Spirit:Adamantoise, The adamantoise spirit grants defence enchantments (in exchanged for speed) and the skill "tough as a turtle" manifesting a giant turtle shell infront of the user to absorb damage. You do not often see a rouge tank, but when you do it is an agility tank aiming to provoke but not be hit, this spirit add`s to your survive-ability, while not a common spirit to acquire in the early stages of the game it would allow you to stand toe to toe with more traditional tanks.
  • Poison dps, Spirit: Kelzmelzer (insectoid species), With it`s poison focus this suits a rouge who specialises in poisons. It`s skill "liquid poison" allows your to coat your weapon or fill a vial with poison to be used anytime. While other spirits have similar uses Kelzmlizer spirits are not hard to come by.
  • Melee dps, Spirit: Fachan, while granting enchanged physical damage (to one hand) it lowers accuracy. This works well with one-handed weapons as it only increases the damage on one hand. The idea is that the damage output makes up for the lower accuracy, a rouge often has higher accuracy then other classes to begin with, so this can be a less risky choice then appears on the face of it.
  • Support, Spirit: Mite, a small beetle. This magical beetle grants the skill "see though mein eyes" which allows the user to see what the target is seeing. While there are many support skills with a more direct impact I feel this has more everyday use for a party, despite the limitations of the skill in regards to who can be targeted.
