Race

Race does not play a large role towards your being a rogue as all races can pick the rogue class. A few notible racial bonuses towards rogue are:

  • Drell - bonuses to camoflage
  • Dark eleves - bonuses to camoflage in the dark
  • Krogan - Unable to sneak
  • Argonian - bonuses to poisons, resitance to poison

Levels

  • Level 0 - 50

    0 - Starting skills unlocked, 10 - Gain spirit eating ability, 30 - basic skills unlocked(cap raised from 0 to 3), 50 - Advance spirit (Plus increase chance in spirit eating sucess).

  • Level 51 - 100

    70 - Unlock Spirit Projection (different from summoned creatures), 80 - intermidate skills unlocked (cap raised from 3 to 6), 100 - Class advancement

  • Class advancement

    As with other classes your advance class choices will depend on your starting class (Rogue), Spirit absorbed (At time of advance). If you pick a unique/quest class you cannot also pick an advanced class. (eg. You cannot be Saint and Vishkanya at the same time.)

Spirits

Spirits are not ghosts, they are not even powerful by themselves. Spirits give access to skills/powers/abilities & spells, this is why rouges choose to let spirits in.

A spirit can be obtained by eating the heart of the prey. While not always sucessful, it can grant the rouge access to that prey`s abilities. Eg. An egeal`s spirit can grant access to the "egeal eyes" skill and other stat boosts. Below are some of a few spirit examples:

Roles

Rouges often make up the physical dammage roles in a party, they usually act as dps both ranged and melee. Rouges often get support skills for an adventureing party and are often seen in this role aswell. Below are a few build examples:

  • Range dps build, Spirit: Grat (seed spiter), With a crossbow/bow any ranged wepons, the Grat bonuses help towards ranged accuracy and speed. It`s special skill to spit seeds from your mouth also count as a physical ranged attack. While not a commonly picked spirit, the advantage is that you always have a ranged wepon even while unarmed, So thoes ranged bonuses don`t go to waste.
  • Tank, Spirit:Adamantoise, The adamantoise spirit grants defence enchantments (in exchanged for speed) and the skill "tough as a turtle" manifiesting a giant turtle shell infrount of the user to absorb dammage. You do not often see a rouge tank, but when you do it is an agility tank aiming to provoke but not be hit, this spirit add`s to your survive-abilty, while not a common spirit to aquire in the early stages of the game it would allow you to stand toe to toe with more triditonal tanks.
  • Poison dps, Spirit: Kelzmelzer (insectoid species), With it`s poison focus this suits a rouge who specalises in poisons. It`s skill "liquid poison" allows your to coat your wepon or fill a vial with poison to be used anytime. While other spirits have similar uses Kelzmlizer spirits are not hard to come by.
  • Melee dps, Spirit: Fachan, while granting enchanged physical damage (to one hand) it lowers accuracy. This works well with one-handed weapons as it only increases the dammage on one hand. The idea is that the dammage output makes up for the lower accuracy, a rouge often has higher accurcy then other classes to begin with, so this can be a less risky choice then appears on the face of it.
  • Support, Spirit: Mite, a small beatle. This magical beatle grants the skill "see though mein eyes" which allows the user to see what the target is seeing. While there are many support skills with a more direct impact I feel this has more everyday use for a party, dispite the limitations of the skill in reguards to who can be targeted.
